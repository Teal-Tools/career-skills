# Reference: Interview Technique (the "grill me" method)

**Status: [SPEC] — flesh this out early. Imported by `career-profile` (deep path),
`performance-self-review`, `career-clarity`, `interview-prep`.**

The adaptive interview is a core interaction pattern (inspired by Matt Pocock's
skills). Extracting it here keeps the four skills that use it DRY.

## Principles to encode
- **One question at a time.** Never dump a list; let each answer shape the next.
- **Dig on vague answers.** "I led the project" → "What did *you* specifically do?
  What broke? What was the outcome, in numbers?"
- **Quantify relentlessly.** Push for metrics, scope, timeframes, dollar/percent impact.
- **Follow energy.** Notice what the user lights up about vs. flattens on — that's
  signal, especially for `career-clarity`.
- **Don't accept the first surface answer.** The good material is usually the second
  or third layer down.
- **Reflect back structured findings**, not raw transcript.
- **Know when to stop.** Enough to fill the deliverable, not an interrogation.
