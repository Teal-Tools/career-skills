# Reference: Context calibration (career stage, industry, education)

Used alongside `references/resume-rubric.md` by `resume-review` and `tailor-to-job`.
The rubric's five dimensions never change — **what a good score looks like does**. A
retail resume doesn't need finance-grade quantification; a new grad leading with
education isn't a structural flaw. Detect the context first, then score against the
right expectations. Context over rules.

## Career stage

**Calculate from work history — never assume from education dates or titles.** Find
the earliest relevant professional start date (exclude retail/food service unless
career-relevant), subtract from the present or the most recent end date, round to
whole years:

| Stage | Years | Section order | Education treatment | Summary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **New Grad** | 0–1 | Contact → Target → **Education** → Experience → Projects → Skills | Prominent: degree, GPA if 3.5+ (omit otherwise, never explain), 3–5 relevant courses, honors | None, or 1–2 targeted lines |
| **Early Career** | 1–5 | Contact → Target → Summary? → Experience → Education → Skills | Condensed after ~1 year of work: degree, school, year; drop GPA after 2 years | Optional, 2–3 lines, one aggregate achievement |
| **Mid-Career** | 6–15 | Contact → Target → Summary → Experience → Education → Certs → Skills | Minimal: degree + school; certifications matter more now | Required (the Blurb); career-level, must not repeat Position-1 bullets |
| **Senior / Exec** | 15+ | Contact → Title → Exec Summary → Experience → Earlier Career (condensed) → Board/Advisory → Education | One line; consider dropping the graduation year (age-discrimination protection) | Required and prominent: P&L, org size, transformation narrative |

**Length and bullet-count targets** (benchmarks, not laws — dense achievement content
earns exceptions):

| Stage | Words | Pages | Bullet distribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| New Grad | 300–400 | 1 max | 2–3 per position |
| Early Career | 350–500 | 1 preferred | Position 1: 4–5 (≥1 metric); others: 2–3 |
| Mid-Career | 350–500 | 1–2 | Position 1: 4–5; positions 2–3: 3; positions 4+: 0–2 or title/dates only |
| Senior | 500–650 | 2 standard | Last 10–15 years in detail; earlier career condensed to 2–3 lines |

**What strong bullets prove shifts by stage:** New Grad — initiative and learning
speed; Early — ownership and measurable contribution; Mid — leadership, business
outcomes, scale; Senior — strategy, transformation, enterprise impact. A senior resume
full of tactical detail scores Dimension 3 down even if every bullet has a metric.

## Industry family

Detect from titles, companies, and skills, then adjust what counts as signal:

| Family | What matters most | Hard rules to respect |
|---|---|---|
| **Tech / knowledge work** | Scale handled (users, requests, data), ownership of technical decisions, categorized skills | Don't list every technology ever touched; links (GitHub, portfolio) only if maintained |
| **Finance** | Deal values, portfolio sizes, licenses (CFA, Series), heavy quantification | Conservative format; vague money language ("improved financial performance") is a red flag |
| **Consulting** | Client impact with a "so what," leadership without authority | **One page, non-negotiable at top firms** — even for partners |
| **Marketing** | Campaign metrics, channel depth over channel breadth, portfolio link | "Posted 3x/week" is activity, not a result |
| **Service / retail** | Sales vs. target, satisfaction scores, volume + accuracy, training others | Keep the format simple and scannable; don't over-engineer |
| **Skilled trades** | Current licenses and certs, safety record, equipment proficiency | **Certifications go at the top**, often before experience; one page standard |
| **Creative** | The portfolio does the heavy lifting | Resume stays plain and parseable; the *portfolio* shows the range (proper portfolio site, never a Drive link) |

## Education profile

**Placement:** education leads for new grads, career changers leveraging a degree, and
prestige-driven fields (consulting, banking) at entry level; it trails after ~2 years
of relevant experience; it shrinks to one line at 10+.

**No formal degree — never omit the section entirely** (a missing section reads as a
red flag). Reframe it instead: a "Professional Development" or "Education & Training"
section carrying certifications, programs, and a learning trajectory — and compensate
with heavier quantification and a summary that establishes credibility through results
("self-taught, 8 years production experience, led platform processing $50M annually").

**Bootcamps:** name the program, include duration/intensity (signals rigor), list the
stack, link the capstone; keep a prior degree listed beneath it.

**Incomplete degrees — never imply completion, don't hide relevant work:** "Completed
90 credits toward B.S. Computer Science" or "B.S. (in progress), expected 2027."
Include if relevant, recent, or substantially complete; omit if it was one semester a
decade ago.

**Online certificates:** only if relevant, recognized, recent, and applied — best shown
with the application attached ("Google PM Certificate, 2025 — applied leading a
$200K migration"). Skip the pile of random course completions.

**Advanced degrees:** MBA/PhD can ride the header ("Jane Smith, MBA") when it's central
to the target role; note they can read as neutral-to-negative for hands-on or startup
roles — placement is a positioning choice, not a default.
